The Illios Curriculum Visualizer was created with JavaScript libraries (d3.js , Ember.js , node.js) with the aim of creating an interactive visualization of complex medical school curriculum for integration into Ilios Curriculum Manager.

On this project our team collaborated remotely with a team of developers and stakeholders at UCSF School of Medicine. Project will be integrated in 15+ Health Professional school programs.

A year-long, joint student-lead research study between UC Irvine Informatics Students and the UC Irvine School of Medicine, IMedEd Initative studied the use of the Ipad in Standarized Clinical Exams. With the end goal of improving patient education and integration of mobile techbology into the exam room.

"While there is a growing push for mobile technology in a clinical setting, technology etiquette, effective usage and technology's effect on patient interaction remain under-examined areas. The project I worked on this summer focused on understanding how iPads can be used for patient education in the clinical setting, with the goal of introducing mobile technology into more areas of patient interaction in an effective and consistent fashion. Our research suggests seamless integration of mobile technology into everyday patient interaction is achievable through directed physician training."
