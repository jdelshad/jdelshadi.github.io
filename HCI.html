Evaluating iPad Usage

In the Clinical Setting

A year-long, joint student-lead research study between UC Irvine Informatics Students and the UC Irvine School of Medicine, IMedEd Initative studied the use of the Ipad in Standarized Clinical Exams. With the end goal of improving patient education and integration of mobile techbology into the exam room.

"While there is a growing push for mobile technology in a clinical setting, technology etiquette, effective usage and technology's effect on patient interaction remain under-examined areas. The project I worked on this summer focused on understanding how iPads can be used for patient education in the clinical setting, with the goal of introducing mobile technology into more areas of patient interaction in an effective and consistent fashion. In general, my role was to ask questions as â€œan outsider," providing a non-clinician view by observing video recordings of iPad usage by doctors during examinations. Our research suggests seamless integration of mobile technology into everyday patient interaction is achievable through directed physician training."

Note Taking | Studying Mobile Working

"Mobile Working" was created as part of Human-Computer Interaction Project, studying student note taking behavior in outdoor, lecture hall and library working.This project centered around examining user experience through dimension of 'doing, looking and thinking' to draw inspiration for feature development in a note taking application.

At the conclusion of the study, we created a rapid Balsamiq mock-up based on our study and principals of HCI learned throughout the quarter.

Tools & Techniques :

  • Adobe Illustrator
  • Balsamiq
  • Qualitative Observation, Interviews
  • Think Alouds

Problem Statment: Quoted from Final Report

In almost all areas of study diagrams, charts, equations or other non-textual representations of information become relevant to establishing an understanding of a topic or subject. Laptop possess the capability to produce these images, and student should be able to use this functionality to incorporate these non-textual representations to incorporate them into lecture, reading or textbook notes. How ever the process of creating these more abstract visualization is they are time consuming and require technical skill and practice with software. When taking notes with a laptop there is not quick way to include sketches in documents students are working on, resulting in lost material.

Our solution proposes a mobile application, that would allow students or lecturers to quickly make sketch on their device and have them automatically incorporated into their document. Most student own touch screen device, using this existing technology to enhance and improve education.
