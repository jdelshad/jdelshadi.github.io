Introduction

    The work described on this page details my role in managing a portfolio of non-technical projects aimed and improving student life on UC Irvine’s campus. These projects involved directing a large staff, creating and implementing year-long and multi year projects, working with administrators and policy makers to effect positive change.

  • As the elected Vice President of 23,000 undergraduates, I served as the chief advocate for all matters affecting student life by managing a portfolio of year-long and multi-year projects aimed at improving the student experience on UC Irvine’s campus.

  • I trained and supervised staff of 85+ students working on projects dedicated to improving student life including volunteerism, accessibility, wellness, conservation and other.

  • Together with a team of four other executives, we created, balanced a yearly budget of approximately a $1.5 million for campus programming and advocacy projects.


UC I Giveback

    For UC I Giveback, our effort began with finding a partnering organization that met our goal to impact a large area of the Orange County and provide undergraduates with the opportunity to build ties to their local community beyond campus.


    THINK Together of Orange County is an organization that works directly with students in elementary and middle schools through after-school programs that reach underserved students. Their programs often recruit UCI students who are looking to gain experience in child education. In order to improve UC I Giveback over its inaugural year, we created a committee consisting of students, THINK Together staff, and UC Irvine event planning staff to foster direct collaboration, buy-in, and understanding between all stakeholders. This committee determined it was necessary to improve training for volunteers, expand and optimize transportation networks, along with other minor improvements.

    Funding

    UC I Giveback was a recent addition to campus programming, meaning funding from the Associated Students budget was nonexistent. Our the event relied completely on sponsorships and donations, requiring negotiations with different parties and modifying the event to meet specific funding requirements. I created a proposal and met with various campus partners including Dining and Hospitality, Parking and Transportation, the Green Initiative Fund, and others to secure grants to sustain our program.

    Logistics and Challenges

    We worked with THINK Together and two bussing companies to schedule transport between UCI and the 48 schools across Orange County. By delegating specific routes and volunteer cohorts to each of 13 core event staff members, I oversaw the successful transport of over 250 volunteers and support staff to their respective service sites, successfully mitigating the impact of unexpected obstacles (including bus delays, a car accident) by maintaining clear lines of communication between well-trained leadership staff.

    Results

    Though the event attracted less volunteers than planned the program reached a record number of schools and local students. Additionally, this event generated thousands of hours of community service while kicking off a services of smaller scale continuous service events. Lastly many participants continued to go back to the school site to volunteer, or began to pursue careers in education.

artLab

    ArtLab is a year-round project that involved quarterly events and a year long advocacy agenda broadly centered at promoting and engaging all students in art and advocating for permanent student art spaces. Going into its third year, artLab was struggling and slated to be canceled. The programs difficulties were caused by a failure to meaningfully engage the artistic student community and lost its effectiveness to promote artists to the general student body. We instead put together a team that conducted outreach to existing clubs and departments dedicated to art, performance, and design. These efforts ‘turned around’ this program and has allowed it to continue till this day.


    Purpose & Planning

    At an institution already equipped with strong programs in STEM education and research, it was critical to create an accessible, student-run space that highlights not only the importance of art and design, but also the interdisciplinary nature of artistic expression. We rebranded artLab’s showcases by developing quarterly themes for each event in order to engage specific artistic communities and boost consistent attendance among the general student body. The events were staged in an open space on an outdoor terrace, between the academic core of campus and student meeting and the main entry point of campus. This design allowed for an accidental encounter with the space, it also allowed created a natural transition between two elements realms of the student experience.

    Results

    Attendance at each quarterly artLab grew by more than double, easily draw anywhere from a hundred to two-hundred students, , and it was Associated Students most visible and popular non-concert program. Initially it took some effort to obtain sponsorship and funding for this event, overtime the event itself attracted more sponsorship and involvement requests than we were able to accommodate. artLab has continued to evolved over the last two years, tackling a variety of social issues and creative motifs. The program continues to thrive today.

Lessons Learned

  • Project Buy-in: Our most successful programs were driven by staff that had “buy-in” to their cause and purpose, giving our team a theme or question like, “ why is parking so unaffordable” was more effective than dictating directly what to do. When this wasn’t possible, it was important project leads to explain why an already-selected course of action is important.
